Welcome to my Japan trip blog!

This is the blog for my (Junnan Shimizu's) trip to Japan! Follow along as I complete my independent study in Japanese agriculture and technology.

This is a work-in-progress project, and I am implementing features slowly, but surely so be patient and enjoy seeing the progress of this blog develop as the days go on.


Use the calender to jump to a specific day or scroll down to start at Day 1 of my adventure. Enjoy!

Unfortunately, the farming industry in Japan is having trouble replacing the current workforce. There was also a TV show recorded about here. This is 1/100 mountaineous rice fields (I still need to translate these).

1-5-25
  • Today, I had breakfast and lunch at my Aunt's house and left for the farm in Morimachi. We arrived around 2:00PM and we were given a tour of the property. There is a large hill/mountain behind the house and we walked along the trail. There was bamboo and trees that were not naturally there.

  • Here, the family consists of two parents and three children of ages 6, 5, and 3. I played with them until dinner time. We ate curry.

1-6-25
  • Today, I woke up early at around 5:50 to eat breakfast. The kids ate as well and left for school around 7 / 7:30.
  • I spent almost the entire morning raking and collecting leaves in the backyard that would be used for fertilizer/compost.
    It did start to rain, however, so in the meantime, I installed a vinyl backsplash to their kitchen.
  • After a quick lunch, I went back outside to get started with the compost process. With the pile of leaves I collected (and other roots, grass, nuts, etc.), I had a process of compacting the pile, adding the crushed soybeans, and adding grass/dirt on top and repeating until the bag of crushed soybeans was empty.
  • I found this large larvae in the grass/dirt which I assume helps decompose the leaves.
  • I also split some logs for the fire stove inside to keep the house warm.
  • After I went to the mall while the mom went to the gym.
  • I also collected eggs from the chickens today once we came back.
1-7-24
  • I started the day at around 6:00 AM with breakfast.
  • My first task of the day was to make spheres out of strips of bamboo to make bundles of pine. From my understanding, these are used to make sake.
  • Next, I dug out a trench on the hill next to the house to help with water runoff since there tended to be a muddy spot.
  • Next, I had to put new paper on the sliding doors. This was a tough task because the paper wanted to roll and the glue was not very sticky. I did not do a great job, but it gets the job done. This took multiple hours, unfortunately.
  • After relaxing for a bit, once the kids returned home I played with them and helped cook dinner.
1-8-24
  • One of the more heavy workload days today. Since Yoko-san was not working at home like she usually does, this was the to-do list I was given.
  • All the work was not very glamorous, so I did not take many pictures; However, I did come across one of the biggest and scariest-looking spiders I had ever seen.
    According to Reddit, this is a Joro Spider (Trichonephila clavata) and is completely harmless.
  • Also, while I was cutting wood, I came across these spiders as well. Also big and scary.
    According to Reddit, these are Huntsman Spiders. They are venemous, but apparently not dangerous to humans.
  • After my chores, I went to 7Eleven for snacks.
1-9-24
  • After breakfast, my first task of the day was to break down these two old garden boxes made of bamboo that were deteriorating.
  • Here at the house, they grow Shiitake Mushrooms!
    I had to move the logs to a different spot from their backyard to the left of their house.
  • Next, I went to harvest some radishes which is about a 5 minute walk away from the house. I picked 11, 10 for my next task, and one for dinner.
  • Once, I got back to the house with the radishes, I cut the leaves off which can be eaten and/or fed to the chickens. Then, I washed the radishes to hang them to make takuwan (pickled daikon radish) as seen in the pictures. This is done by first drying the radishes for 1-3 weeks and then prepping with salts and such.
    The hanging process was not easy -- in fact, I dropped one and it split in half.
  • Next, I did a couple of chores with Yoko-san. First, we went to a tofu making place. Because it was garbage day, all the crushed soybeans that were used to make tofu are left out to be thrown out. This family goes to this tofu making place to collect their scraps (with their permission). These scraps are just soybeans, so they can be eaten; however, we use this as part of the chicken feed which is a mix of this and seeds. Yoko-san was also telling me that they go to sushi and fish prepping places for scraps which also make great feed for the chickens.
  • Next, we went to a couple more stores to pick up some things for dinner. This place specifically stood out to me because it is a market where all the goods are local and made and sold by farmers in the area.
  • Because we were out, we weant to a Ramen place for lunch.
  • Once we were back, we went straight back to work. We first went over the hill that surrounds the house to access some bamboo. Here we collected two large stalks of bamboo, cut off the branches, and carried them back to the house.
  • The purpose of this bamboo is to remake the clothes hangers outside because it was rotting. This is what I want to make, but slightly smaller.
    To make this, I cut the bamboo into 6 even pieces, and used the old string used for the previous one (and braided the fraying ends) and tied them together. The last picture is the finished product.
  • After, I fed the chickens and picked up the eggs. They laid 7 eggs today.
  • Gin-chan and Yu-chan asked this morning for me to be there to pick them up from preschool/daycare, so I went. The place was very cute. They have a showcase for the day's lunch which I thought was cool
  • We had stew for dinner with the things we bought during the day and had chocolate fudge with ice cream and berries because it was Yoko-san's birthday!

  • After this, I trimmed some of the bushes around the property. Subtle, but enough I think.
  • In front of the house, there were lots of leaves, scraps, and dirt that I swept away. I collected the leaves and moved it to the composting bins, and the dust I swept to the grass.
  • Lastly, I did my other everday chores like put the laundry away, feed the chickens, and collect the eggs. Here is a picture of the food I give the chickens. It is a mixtureof the tofu soybean scraps and seeds.
